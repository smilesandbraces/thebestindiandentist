Frequently Asked Questions

What is tartar and why does it need to be removed?

Tartar (dental calculus) is hardened plaque that forms on teeth over time:contentReference[oaicite:26]{index=26}. Unlike soft plaque, tartar cannot be brushed away. Removing it is important because tartar bacteria cause gum inflammation, cavities, and bad breath:contentReference[oaicite:27]{index=27}. Professional cleaning by a dentist safely eliminates tartar and keeps your teeth healthy.

Is tartar removal painful?

Most patients feel little or no pain during routine cleaning. The process should not be painful:contentReference[oaicite:28]{index=28}. If you have sensitive gums or a lot of buildup, we can use topical anesthetic. Our goal is a comfortable, quick visit that leaves you feeling great.

How often should I get a cleaning to remove tartar?

For most people, a professional cleaning every six months is recommended:contentReference[oaicite:29]{index=29}. Some patients with fast buildup may need visits more frequently. We’ll help you determine the right schedule based on your oral health.
