5 Expert Tips for Maintaining Oral Hygiene

Aug 1, 2025 • —

On this page

  1. Brush the right way (2× daily)
  2. Clean between teeth
  3. Tongue cleaning & mouthwash
  4. Diet, timing & hydration
  5. If you wear braces or aligners
  6. Your simple daily routine

1) Brush the right way (twice daily, 2 minutes)

Use a **soft-bristle** brush and **fluoride toothpaste**. Angle the bristles 45° to the gumline (modified **Bass technique**), use short, gentle strokes, and cover all surfaces: outer, inner and chewing. Don’t scrub aggressively—let the bristles do the work.

  • 2 minutes total: 30 seconds per quadrant
  • Pea-sized fluoride paste for adults; rice-grain for kids
  • Replace the brush head every 3 months or after illness
  • Electric brushes with a **pressure sensor** help prevent over-brushing
  • Brush the **gumline**—that’s where plaque hides
  • Finish by lightly brushing the palate and cheek sides

2) Clean between teeth (daily)

Toothbrush bristles can’t reach tight contacts. Use **floss** (slide along the tooth in a gentle “C” shape) or **interdental brushes** (great for wider spaces and around braces). Water flossers are a helpful add-on for those with dexterity issues or dental work.

3) Don’t skip the tongue & consider mouthwash

Most bad breath originates on the **tongue**. Use a scraper or your brush to remove residue from back to front. An **alcohol-free fluoride mouthwash** can reduce bacterial load and strengthen enamel—swish for 30 seconds, but avoid rinsing with water immediately after.

4) Diet, timing & hydration matter

Rule of frequency: It’s not only what you eat, it’s how often. Frequent snacking or sugary drinks keep mouth pH low and fuel cavities. Keep sweet/acidic items to mealtimes and rinse with water after.
  • Limit sticky sweets, sports drinks and frequent sipping
  • Chew **sugar-free xylitol** gum after meals to stimulate saliva
  • Drink plenty of water; dry mouth increases decay risk

5) If you wear braces or aligners

  • Use **interdental brushes** under wires and around brackets
  • Aligner users: rinse trays with cool water every time you remove them; brush trays gently (no hot water)
  • Avoid hard, sticky foods that can dislodge brackets

Your simple daily routine

  1. Brush (2 minutes) with fluoride toothpaste
  2. Floss or use interdental brushes
  3. Tongue cleaning
  4. Optional: fluoride mouthwash (don’t rinse with water after)
