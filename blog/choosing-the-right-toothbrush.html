How to Choose the Right Toothbrush & Toothpaste

July 15, 2025 • —

1. Start with the bristles — Soft always wins

Soft bristles gently reach under the gumline to remove plaque effectively without harming enamel. Medium or hard bristles can lead to gum recession and sensitivity. If you tend to brush aggressively, go for a brush with a pressure sensor or a flexible neck.

2. Head size and handle design

  • Pick a small to medium head that can reach molars easily.
  • Choose a non-slip ergonomic handle for better control.
  • A built-in tongue cleaner is a great add-on.

3. Manual vs Electric Toothbrush

Manual

  • Affordable and easily available.
  • Perfect if your brushing technique is already excellent.

Electric (oscillating or sonic)

  • Comes with a 2-minute timer and pressure sensor.
  • Ideal for people with braces, implants, or limited dexterity.

Our clinic often recommends a good-quality electric toothbrush for predictable and consistent oral hygiene — especially during orthodontic treatments.

4. Which toothpaste should you choose?

  • Fluoride: 1000–1500 ppm for adults; a smear for under-3s, a pea-sized amount for ages 3–6.
  • Sensitivity relief: look for stannous fluoride or potassium nitrate.
  • Whitening: avoid abrasive pastes if you have receding gums or sensitive teeth.
  • Tartar control: use with professional advice to prevent over-drying tissues.

5. For braces, aligners, and implants

  • Braces: use orthodontic brush heads and interdental brushes.
  • Aligners: always brush before reinserting trays and clean aligners with cool water.
  • Implants: clean gently around implant crowns using super-floss or soft brushes.

6. Replace your brush regularly

Switch to a new brush every three months or sooner if the bristles splay. Replace immediately after an illness to avoid reinfection.

Bottom line: A soft-bristled toothbrush with a small head, used for two minutes twice daily alongside fluoride toothpaste, keeps your smile healthy. Remember — technique matters more than tools.
